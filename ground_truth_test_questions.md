# Suggested Ground-Truth Questions for Evaluation

## Q1
Question: What do students say about WMU CS program quality for transfer students?
Expected answer: The sentiment is mixed but generally reassuring. One commenter notes staffing concerns but says the issues are not worse than comparable universities, and recommends WMU for facilities, cost, transfer convenience, and success stories.
Relevant sources: 02_wmu_cs_program_quality_transfer_thread.txt, 06_wmu_engineering_cs_success_stories.txt

## Q2
Question: How should a student find old CS syllabi for transfer credit?
Expected answer: They should try professors, but also contact the department administrative office. They may need the syllabus from the same semester or academic year when they took the course.
Relevant source: 04_wmu_cs_syllabus_retrieval_advice.txt

## Q3
Question: What do students say about WMU dining options and hours?
Expected answer: Students describe dining as generally acceptable or good, with decent variety. Valley and Bistro are mentioned positively, but Bistro hours are criticized because it closes early and is closed on weekends. Draper Carryout is mentioned as useful for grab-and-go food.
Relevant source: 07_wmu_dining_food_situation.txt

## Q4
Question: Is the meal plan worth it for freshmen living in the dorms?
Expected answer: Some students recommend having a meal plan in the dorms, especially for convenience. Unlimited is described as useful for frequent snacks and social visits, but students should compare the price difference. A 14-meal plan may run out before the week ends for some students.
Relevant source: 08_wmu_meal_plan_value.txt

## Q5
Question: Which dorms are mentioned as not requiring a meal plan?
Expected answer: Britton/Hadley Hall in Valley 1 is repeatedly mentioned as the main freshman option where a meal plan is not required.
Relevant source: 09_wmu_dorms_without_required_meal_plan.txt

## Q6
Question: What are common complaints about WMU parking?
Expected answer: Complaints include permit cost, full lots, long walks, empty reserved employee spaces, and difficulty moving between main campus and Parkview quickly.
Relevant source: 11_wmu_parking_situation_and_costs.txt

## Q7
Question: Does WMU parking enforcement completely stop after the semester?
Expected answer: Not completely. Permit rules may change during specific break windows, but non-permit violations such as accessibility spaces, reserved spaces, tow zones, short-term spaces, meters, and unmarked spaces can still be enforced.
Relevant source: 12_wmu_parking_enforcement_rules_discussion.txt

## Q8
Question: What housing red flags were reported in the CCATK apartment discussion?
Expected answer: A commenter reported run-down interiors, mold in vents, flooding risk, garbage, rats, stray animals, high utilities, and dirty pool conditions. This is one user's account and should be verified with current sources.
Relevant source: 13_wmu_off_campus_apartment_warning_ccatk.txt

## Q9
Question: What career support is mentioned for engineering or CS-adjacent students?
Expected answer: One source mentions that the College of Engineering and Applied Sciences hosts career fairs each fall and spring, and that career services can help with resumes and interviews. Another source mentions alumni-style success stories from WMU CS/engineering.
Relevant sources: 10_wmu_exchange_student_food_housing_career_fair.txt, 06_wmu_engineering_cs_success_stories.txt

## Q10 - likely failure / ambiguity case
Question: What is the single best WMU dining hall?
Expected answer: The dataset does not support one definitive answer. It includes positive comments about Valley and Bistro, but the answer depends on location, hours, variety, and student preference.
Relevant source: 07_wmu_dining_food_situation.txt
Suggested accuracy judgment if system overstates one answer: Partially accurate or inaccurate due to overgeneralization from limited comments.
